My Journey to Dental School
Freshman Year
Summer before Senior Year

How can you create your dental school list when applying?

  1. I applied to 19 schools and received interviews to 3 of them
  2. Some schools have higher stats than others, yet what was surprising is getting interviews from schools with higher stats. Hence, it depends on what the schools are really looking for
  3. I did not buy the ADEA official guide to dental schools and I could not burrow it from anyone as I was not in the US. Hence, I searched every school individually and wrote down their stats
  4. My goal was to become a dentist in the US no matter what State they are in. So I applied to every possible school that would accept internationals, that did not care about my religion and that did not require Anatomy as it was not offered in CMU Qatar
  5. An important thing to consider is that every school first looks at its residents, then non- residents, and then internationals
  6. Apply to schools that you are competitive for, apply for schools that you can afford, and apply to some dream schools that if you got in, it would be amazing but you do not expect to go there

What have I done during my gap year before Dental School?

  1. I have been working as a Research Assistant at CMUQ since my graduation (May) to pay back my Financial Aid and gain more experience in the lab while working on my Honors Thesis project
  2. My PI was very flexible, he allowed me to take a few weeks off when I needed to travel twice to the US for my 3 interviews (September- December)
  3. I started developing a Dental-related website, readental.me and currently working on writing about my experiences that have led me to where I am today to help other international students with similar interests
  4. Always make sure that you are productive that you can talk about during your interviews and try to develop certain skills that you wouldn’t have otherwise
  5. Joined a 48hr competition at CMUQ with my friends and won an iPad Pro

  7. HAVE FUN because I know I may not when school starts!

How and where did I travel to my Dental School interviews?

  1. I was extremely lucky to have a supportive father who was willing to pay for all my traveling expenses without any hesitation
  2. I took about 2-3 months to prepare myself for any interview that would come up
  3. I took a lot of notes whether it is about the dental field, interview tips, questions to ask schools, Professors and even students
  4. I also wrote down questions like “Why dentistry, tell me about yourself, why do you want to join this school, what are your weaknesses and strengths”
  5. I tried to answer these questions over the 2 months period and tried to give different stories or examples
  6. At the end, I tried to shorten my answers to make them concise and to have a brief idea of what I want to talk about. BUT DO NOT MEMORIZE ANSWERS! Always be yourself
  7. I practiced with friends, family, career advisor, talked to myself in the mirror and even on the airplanes and with my Uber drivers (who eventually became my friends)
  8. For my first interview at Maryland, I travelled from Qatar with a checked-in bag and arrived ~3 nights before my interview to avoid jet lag during the interview day. I also planned to stay at Virginia for 2 weeks in case I got any interviews around that time. So I left Maryland the morning after my interview
  9. Luckily, I was able to change the date of an interview at Colorado. So I booked for my flights and hotel and traveled after 3 days of reaching to Virginia. I travelled with just a handbag and stayed for just 1 night, then flew back to Virginia the night of my interview day to my sister before flying to Qatar
  10. I took my travel handbag with my to the University. They were nice enough to provide a place to off my luggage
  11. I received my acceptance to Maryland ~Dec. 3rd and 2 days later I received my third interview invitation at Tufts
  12. It was extremely difficult weighing out the advantages and disadvantages of each university and whether or not it would be worth paying so much money for a flight to the US within 4 days and finding a hotel around campus, not knowing how it will end up and whether or not I will be accepted into Tufts, considering that it was quite competitive and an extremely expensive University
  13. I traveled to Boston with just a handbag ~2 nights before my interview and flew back the morning after my interview day
  14. It was quite cold in Boston, so I brought a coat which I took off and kept into a room as soon as I entered the building
  15. I carried my bag which had my passport and IDs as well as gum, lipstick, perfume, floss, toothpaste and a toothbrush as you may need them after lunch
  16. I also carried my brown Portfolio which had a card with my email and contact information just in case the interviewer asked me for it. It also had a copy of my website, my CV, a pen and a notepad to take notes during the presentations given before the interview
  17. Some schools may give you pens, notepads and booklets about their university which can sometimes be useful to read to be able to answer the “Why us?”. However, I do recommend doing your own research before the interview day to avoid stress. You can look at them after your interview when you get to choose between different universities
  18. For transportations, I used Uber as I was new to all these places and did not know anyone living there
  19. The days before the interview I try to walk around the city, destress, think about the reasons why I would love to spend the next 4 years of my life there and check out places or cafes to work at and review my notes
  20. I also tried to have fun and pretend like it was just a trip for entertainment and exploration

How did I get in?

  1. Be prepared
  2. Self-confidence and believing that I can get in just like everyone else did even when several people told me not to waste my money and time on something almost impossible
  3. Finding something unique in yourself, different, minor in Computational Biology
  4. Doing and showing them things others did not (website)
  5. Tell them why you would be a good addition to their program and university as a whole
  6. Show them you are continuously learning, growing and seeking new knowledge, rather than just waiting for them to accept you
  7. Persistence and resilience
  8. If your GPA is not as high as the University’s average GPA, then always aim for high DAT scores (20+). However, do not be discouraged if either of them are average or even below average
  9. My GPA was average and so was my my DAT score, a 19
  10. People told me to re-take the DAT, however, I had to ensure I was completely ready to retake it and score higher. Because, as an International student, I had to think about the costs of booking another flight to the US, living expenses and hotel bookings
  11. In such cases, focus on what makes you unique and what your strengths are if they are not grades
  12. BE YOURSELF, HAVE FUN and MAKE LONG-LASTING FRIENDS

What is one thing if I would go back I would change?

  1. Shadow more dentists earlier to learn more about the field, make sure this is really what you want to do and to show your commitment
  2. Do independent Research to experience something that will help you later on in Medical or Dental school and definitely in graduate schools
  3. Focus more on learning and grades will automatically follow
  4. Get close to your Professors and get to know them and for them to know you more, because you will need recommendation letters from them
  5. Work as a Course Assistant to teach younger students
  6. Prepare for my DAT earlier
  7. Write my Personal Statement earlier
  8. Apply for several internships in the summer to make sure you get into at least one of them
  9. Be an active member of the community
  10. Have leadership experiences, try to be part of something new and help in building it
  11. Try to learn more about yourself and what you actually want to do in the future, set your goals and follow your dream no matter what people tell you
  12. Make sure you are unique in the applicant pool
  13. Volunteer abroad or be part of a service trip if not the organizer

What are the most frequently asked Q’s during interviews?

  1. What are your 3 strengths and 3 weaknesses?
  2. What can you add to the program?
  3. Why dentistry?
  4. Tell me about yourself
  5. Tell me about your family if you had mentioned someone in your application
  6. Who is your role model?
  7. If not dentistry what would you do?
  8. Why not become a nurse or a doctor to help people?
  9. How can your current work help you?
  10. What leadership skills do you have and what service have you provided your community?
  11. Where else have you applied?
  12. Why would you choose to join our university and not any of the other 22 universities you applied to?
  13. Where have you previously travelled?
  14. What extracurricular activities have you done?
  15. What do you plan on specializing in?
  16. How can you explain your breast cancer research to a patient?
